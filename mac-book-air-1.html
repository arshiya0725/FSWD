Biometric authentication is a security method that uses unique physical or behavioral characteristics to verify and authenticate individuals' identities. It relies on biological traits that are inherent to each person, making them distinct and difficult to replicate. These characteristics can include fingerprints, facial features, iris patterns, voice, and even behavioral patterns like typing rhythm or gait.
